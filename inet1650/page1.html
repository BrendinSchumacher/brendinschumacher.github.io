Classes

Information

There are a total of ten classes in Dark Souls III. Then with each class there are different presets and abilities. If you are a new player then for most games that are atleast similar to this you want to start with the knight. The knight class is the easiest to master and maintain. If you were a veteran to the game then you would probably go for the deprived. The deprived is a minimalist character you start off with almost nothing and very bsic equpment.

Stats

In addition to having different equpment they all have different stats. There are a total of ten stats and as you level up you place points into them according to your class. For example a knight would more than likely want to place most of their points in streangth, dextarity, and vitality. The reason for this is so that they have high sustainability in addition to being able handle heavy sheilds and swords. As for a sorcerer you would need to place those points in intelligence, faith, and attunement. All of those contribut to the level and sevarity of the spell that they cast.

Conclusion

So to pick between all of these classes and how to build them can either take up a lot of time to reserch or to just understand how each one would benifite eachother. I would recomend the later because in the end you are more succesful and your character is exactally how you would want it. Instead of emulating another persons preferences of theirs.
